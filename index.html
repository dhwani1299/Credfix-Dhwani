The Brief

One product. Two audiences. One shot to earn their trust.

Credfix users arrive scared. Missed EMIs, collection calls, a loan rejection they did not see coming. The product's job is not to overwhelm them with data. It is to make them feel like someone is in their corner.

This submission is built around one insight: clarity and calm are conversion levers, not just UX principles.

Question 01

4 screens. Each one earns the next tap.

Users will pay Rs.99 if they feel the cost of not knowing is higher. Every screen builds that feeling through precision, not pressure. Dark UI was chosen deliberately. Users in financial stress experience lower cognitive load on dark interfaces. The amber accent always points toward resolution.

Design Decisions

Every choice has a reason.

Decision 01
Dark UI over light
Users arriving from a loan rejection or collection call are in high stress. Dark interfaces measurably reduce cognitive load in anxious states. A light UI would feel clinical and cold for this audience.
Reduces drop-off at onboarding
Decision 02
Strategic blur — count visible, content hidden
The locked cards reveal severity counts but not content. This creates specific curiosity rather than vague mystery. Users know something is wrong before they pay, making Rs.99 feel protective rather than predatory.
Higher subscription conversion intent
Decision 03
Value before price on paywall
Three feature tiles appear before the Rs.99 price. The crossed-out "Rs.499 elsewhere" anchors perception. By the time users see the price, they have already mentally accepted the value.
Reduces paywall abandonment
Decision 04
Score ring as the hero element
The gradient arc, glowing marker, and spectral range bar make the score feel like a precision instrument. This builds trust in the data before any action is asked of the user.
Increases perceived product credibility
Decision 05
Red palette shift on fix screen
Screen 4 uses a deep red hero, a deliberate palette break from the navy base. It signals the user has entered fix mode. This visual cue increases task completion on the most important screen in the flow.
Higher CTA tap rate on fix screen
Decision 06
Numbered steps over bullet points
Numbered steps with amber gradient badges reduce decision fatigue. The user knows there are exactly 3 things to do, in sequence. Numbers feel like a plan, which is exactly what a stressed user needs.
Reduces support queries post-unlock
The Gap We Noticed

Credfix's content barely reaches the people who need it most.

Credfix's LinkedIn posts get low engagement, a pattern common to fintech brands that post product features instead of genuine financial insight. The people who need credit repair are not on LinkedIn looking for app updates.

They are on Instagram, saving posts their friends shared, watching content that explains why their loan got rejected. The content has to meet them there, in their format, in their language.

The two carousels below are built to do exactly that. Not promote the app. Earn the audience first.

The emotional carousel meets users where they feel pain. The data carousel earns reach from people who do not yet know they need Credfix.

The Thinking

Constraints, decisions, and the thread connecting it all.

Core Insight
Design for the emotion, not the feature.
Credfix's competitors, OneScore and Oolka, compete on features. Free score, notifications, report simplification. Credfix cannot win that fight at Rs.99 unless users feel the product is standing with them. Every screen and every carousel slide was built around one question: does this make the user feel understood?
Design Constraints
What shaped every decision.
  • Users are in high-anxiety states, so no alarming language and no red-heavy UI on the primary flow
  • Rs.99 must feel like protection, not a paywall, so value framing must precede price every time
  • The blur must show enough to create urgency, not so much that users feel manipulated
  • Instagram content must use only verified data, no invented stats ever
  • The fix detail screen is the highest-stakes screen in the app and must reduce cognitive load, not add to it
  • Both carousels must work without the Credfix brand visible, earn trust first, reveal the product second
Business Impact
How this connects to Credfix's growth.
The app flow is built to maximise the only conversion that matters: free user to Rs.99 subscriber. The paywall design with value tiles, anchor pricing, and amber CTA is a conversion rate optimization decision, not just a visual one.
The two carousels serve different funnel stages. The emotional carousel converts warm audiences. The data carousel creates cold reach, earning shares from people who have never heard of Credfix, pulling them into the funnel, and letting the product do the rest.
The Content Gap
Why better content is a growth lever, not just marketing.
119 million Indians are now monitoring their credit score, a 51% jump in one year. 77% of them are Gen Z and Millennials actively looking for help. Credfix's current content does not reach them in the format they consume.
The two carousels here are a content strategy argument: educational, shareable Instagram content is the cheapest acquisition channel available, if the content is good enough that people save it, share it, and tag someone in it without being asked.
Why It Works

Design that moves numbers, not just pixels.

Each deliverable is connected to a business outcome. These are not decorative choices. They are conversion, retention, and awareness decisions expressed through design.
